# Guild Reference Sheets

Quick-reference for each playable guild.

---

## 🗝️ Thieves' Guild

**"We're very professional. We've got a receipt."**

### Ability: Shadowy Acquisition
- **Cost:** 2 Gold per use
- **Effect:** Steal 1 influence token from an adjacent district and place it in a district you're in
- **Limit:** Once per turn

### Strategy
- Focus on border districts (easier to steal from neighbors)
- Target weak districts (1-2 enemy tokens)
- The Shades is your home turf — high risk, high reward

### Strengths
- Flexible positioning
- Can deny opponents control
- Good at disruption

### Weaknesses
- Higher Watch attention (pay +1 gold on fines)
- Need adjacent districts to steal from
- Requires gold to use ability

### Starting Position
- Place tokens in The Shades + The Hub

---

## ⚔️ Assassins' Guild

**"We don't kill people. We inhumate them. There's a difference. Paperwork."**

### Ability: Professional Elimination
- **Cost:** 4 Gold per use
- **Effect:** Remove 1 enemy agent/token from any district on the board
- **Limit:** Once per turn

### Strategy
- Control high-value districts (Patrician's Palace, Unseen University)
- Eliminate threats before they grow
- Save ability for critical moments

### Strengths
- Precision removal (anywhere on board)
- Can break enemy control
- Intimidation factor

### Weaknesses
- Expensive ability (need steady gold income)
- No defensive bonuses
- Target of retaliation

### Starting Position
- Place tokens in Patrician's Palace + Pseudopolis Yard

---

## 💋 Seamstresses' Guild

**"We do very respectable work. Very respectable."**

### Ability: Enhanced Income
- **Cost:** Passive (no cost)
- **Effect:** Gain +1 gold per district you control (during Dawn phase)
- **Limit:** Always active

### Strategy
- Rapid expansion (more districts = more gold)
- Avoid conflict (income > combat)
- Accumulate gold for Wealth Victory

### Strengths
- Fastest gold accumulation
- No action cost for ability
- Economic powerhouse

### Weaknesses
- Fragile (easily displaced by Assassins/Thieves)
- No defensive or offensive abilities
- Relies on controlling many districts

### Starting Position
- Place tokens in The Hippo + The Docks (safe + profitable)

---

## 🪙 Beggars' Guild

**"Spare a copper, guv'nor? Or several gold pieces. Whichever."**

### Ability: Immune to Authority
- **Cost:** Passive (no cost)
- **Effect:** Never pay Watch fines. Immune to all Watch raids.
- **Limit:** Always active

### Strategy
- Operate freely in high-danger districts (The Shades, The Docks)
- Take risks others can't afford
- Slow and steady (low resources but safe)

### Strengths
- No Watch penalties ever
- Can hold dangerous districts without cost
- Unpredictable to opponents

### Weaknesses
- Lower starting gold (start with 3 instead of 5)
- No offensive abilities
- Slower expansion

### Starting Position
- Place tokens in The Shades + The Watch House (embrace danger)

---

## 🏛️ Merchants' Guild

**"Buy low, sell high, complain constantly."**

### Ability: Favorable Trade
- **Cost:** Passive (no cost)
- **Effect:** When trading with other players, exchange at 2:1 ratio instead of 1:1
  - Example: Trade 2 gold for 1 favor (normally 3:1)
- **Limit:** Always active

### Strategy
- Broker deals between players
- Accumulate favors efficiently
- Complete high-favor contracts

### Strengths
- Best trading rates in the game
- Can convert gold to favors cheaply
- Diplomatic powerhouse

### Weaknesses
- No combat abilities
- Relies on other players willing to trade
- Vulnerable to aggression

### Starting Position
- Place tokens in The Docks + The Hub (trade centers)

---

## 🧪 Alchemists' Guild *(Optional/Advanced)*

**"It's perfectly safe. Probably. Mostly."**

### Ability: Experimental Science
- **Cost:** 3 Gold per use
- **Effect:** Roll 1d6:
  - **1:** Catastrophic failure (lose 2 influence from any district)
  - **2-3:** Minor setback (lose 1 gold)
  - **4-5:** Success (place 2 influence in any district)
  - **6:** Critical success (place 3 influence + gain 1 favor)
- **Limit:** Once per turn

### Strategy
- High-risk, high-reward plays
- Best when desperate or ahead (nothing to lose / can absorb losses)
- Embrace chaos

### Strengths
- Potentially strongest ability (3 influence on roll of 6)
- Unpredictable (hard to plan against)
- Fun to play

### Weaknesses
- 50% chance of negative outcome
- Can backfire spectacularly
- Requires gold investment with no guarantee

### Starting Position
- Place tokens in Unseen University + The Hub (magical chaos fits)

## Guild Selection Tips

**New Players:**
- Seamstresses' Guild (simple, economic)
- Beggars' Guild (forgiving, safe)

**Experienced Players:**
- Thieves' Guild (tactical positioning)
- Assassins' Guild (aggressive control)

**Negotiators:**
- Merchants' Guild (diplomacy)

**Chaos Lovers:**
- Alchemists' Guild (risk/reward)

---

## Guild Interactions

**Best Allies:**
- Merchants + Anyone (they want to trade)
- Beggars + Thieves (operate in dangerous areas)

**Natural Rivals:**
- Assassins vs. Everyone (they eliminate people)
- Seamstresses vs. Thieves (Thieves steal from them)

**Wildcard:**
- Alchemists (could help or hurt anyone, including themselves)
